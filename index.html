Hamilton Junior Naturalist Club

Hamilton Junior Naturalist Club, known as "Junats" began in 1962. It is an active club for 10-18 year olds interested in all aspects of natural history and preservation in New Zealand.

About the club

Hamilton Junior Naturalist Club, (HJNC) or Junats for short, is for young people from the age of 10 to 18, who have enthusiasm for some aspect of natural history and the outdoors.

Children, young adults and family groups have been meeting new friends and learning about the natural world around them with JUNATS since 1961. The club built and owns Te Kauri Lodge at Oparau, Kawhia, and is active in the restoration of Te Kauri Park Reserve. If you are interested in the sciences, natural history, the outdoors and enjoy meeting other people with similar interests then this could be the Club you have been looking for! Come along to one of our Friday night presentations to meet and chat with us about who we are and what we do.

Te Kauri Lodge

Te Kauri Lodge, only an hour from Hamilton is the gateway to 1,100ha of native bush, 500 year-old kauri, stunning karst landscapes, wetlands and wild life, providing an outdoor experience that is hard to beat!

Te Kauri Lodge, owned and managed by us, is available for hire all year round. It is available to schools, youth groups, adult groups and organisations, orienteering and tramping clubs, who have an interest in natural history, environmental education, and enjoy the New Zealand bush.

Te Kauri Lodge Museum

Junats has one of the best little museums in NZ located right here at Te Kauri Lodge.

Our museum is brainchild of our very own historian/archaeologist/cave explorer/naturalist Chris Templer, and was built by Chris along with a dedicated team of Junat volunteers.
